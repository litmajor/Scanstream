## 📊 The Three Interaction Models

### **Model 1: Full Autonomy (Hands-Off)**

```
You: Set initial parameters
  ↓
Agents: Do everything independently
  ↓
You: Watch leaderboard, see results
  ↓
System: Makes all decisions (spawn, retire, trade execution)
```

**Pros:**
- Minimal time investment
- System discovers emergent strategies
- Agents optimize themselves
- True "algorithmic trading"

**Cons:**
- You're not really engaged
- Loss of control
- Can't course-correct
- Feels passive
- System goes rogue (theoretically)

**Real-world example:** Set a cron job and check it monthly

---

### **Model 2: Full Manual Control (Hands-On)**

```
You: Review every agent decision
  ↓
You: Approve/reject trades
  ↓
You: Manually spawn/retire agents
  ↓
You: Adjust parameters daily
```

**Pros:**
- Total control
- You understand every decision
- Can intervene when wrong
- Most engaging

**Cons:**
- You're doing all the work
- Defeats the purpose of agents
- Becomes your job
- Exhausting
- You become the bottleneck

**Real-world example:** Manual trading with extra steps

---

### **Model 3: Strategic Delegation (Hybrid/Optimal)**

```
You: Set strategic direction
  ↓
Agents: Execute autonomously within guardrails
  ↓
System: Proposes major changes (new agents, new strategies)
  ↓
You: Approve/reject key decisions
  ↓
System: Learns from your preferences & optimizes
```

**Pros:**
- Agents do 90% of work
- You make the 10% that matters
- Feels like leadership, not management
- Engaging without being exhausting
- You're steering, not piloting
- System adapts to your preferences

**Cons:**
- Requires clear decision framework
- Need to check in regularly
- System needs to know which decisions need approval

**Real-world example:** CEO of agent army, not micromanager
